01 / Logo Decision

One brand. Two roles.

The strongest direction is to treat the brand as a system. The top-right mark becomes the premium public identity. The bottom-left direction becomes the formal document lockup. Together, they give the firm a polished external presence and a practical legal-document standard.

Primary identity

Top-right mark

Use for website, advertising, signage, presentation covers, business cards, social profiles, and premium client-facing material. It has the clearest sense of stature.

Formal lockup

Bottom-left document mark

Use for retainers, ID verification forms, invoices, Word templates, letterhead, closing packages, and administrative documents. It reads as stable and official.

Keep

The crest/mark, serif authority, and gold/navy prestige.

Refine

Spacing, small-size legibility, LLP placement, and slogan lockup.

Systemize

Primary, horizontal, document, icon-only, black, white, and mono versions.

02 / Colour

Quiet confidence.

This palette avoids the loudness of “marketing blue” and the cliché of generic legal burgundy. It is high-contrast, printable, digital-ready, and mature.

03 / Typography

Formal, but not old.

The firm needs a legal voice that feels precise and premium. Serif for judgement and institutional weight. Sans-serif for clarity, forms, email, and digital systems.

Display / Headings
Precision in judgement.

Use a refined serif for headlines, covers, proposal titles, and brand moments. The feeling should be editorial, not ornamental.

Body / Interface
Clear. Structured. Fast.

Use an Apple-system sans-serif stack for documents, web UI, captions, email signatures, and client forms. It keeps the client experience easy to read.

04 / Voice

A voice built for confidence.

The firm voice should be calm, exact, and useful. It should not over-sell. It should reduce uncertainty, explain the next step, and give clients confidence that their matter is being handled with discipline.

Principle

Precise

Use exact words. Show process. Reduce ambiguity. Legal clients are buying clarity under pressure.

Principle

Trusted

Sound calm, experienced, and measured. Avoid hype. Confidence is quieter than marketing.

Principle

Outcome-led

Explain what happens next, what documents are needed, and what the client can expect.

05 / Document Architecture

The identity becomes operational.

This is where the brand becomes useful. The firm should have editable Word templates that feel premium, remain consistent, and are simple for the team to reuse without breaking the design.
